# 📊 Supply Chain ESG & Financial Impact Analytics

## 📌 Business Problem
Modern companies are under increasing pressure to balance sustainability performance (ESG) with financial outcomes. Yet it’s not always clear how ESG practices within the **supply chain** affect financial performance metrics like revenue generation, asset efficiency, profitability, and pricing strategy. This project analyzes how core ESG and financial indicators interact across firms.

ESG here refers to environmental, social, and governance performance — a framework widely used to assess sustainability and risk factors in business operations. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## 🎯 Objective
Assess how ESG-related measures in supply chain context (e.g., asset turnover, profitability, inventory efficiency, R&D investment, advertising spending) relate to financial performance and strategic decision-making.

---

## 📍 Data & Approach

### 📊 Data
- Firm financial and performance indicators including:
  - Net profit margin (NPM)
  - Asset turnover
  - Inventory turnover
  - R&D and advertising ratios
- Supply chain ESG proxy variables from sector revenue and ESG activities

(*The dataset was cleaned, grouped, and aggregated by year and firm key for consistent analysis.*) :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

### 🛠 Methodology
1. **Data Cleaning & Aggregation**
   - Removed missing values & outliers
   - Aggregated by firm and year

2. **Feature Engineering**
   - Created financial ratios to capture profitability and operational efficiency

3. **Exploratory Analysis**
   - Visual and statistical comparisons between ESG indicators and financial outcomes
   - Investigated correlations between profitability, turnover, innovation (R&D), and advertising spend

4. **Cross-Sectional Insights**
   - Analyzed how ESG-related variables interact with financial performance patterns

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- **Profitability & Turnover:** Firms with higher asset turnover and profitability tend to demonstrate better relative ESG-linked performance patterns, suggesting operational efficiency enhances both financial and sustainability signals.  
- **Investment & Innovation:** R&D to sales ratios and advertising spend provide nuanced interpretations of strategic action — innovation investment often aligns with long-term competitive advantage even if short-term metrics vary.  
- **Inventory Efficiency:** A high inventory turnover ratio generally corresponds to stronger demand or more efficient stock management — a core operational performance signal.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

✔ **ESG-aware Financial Planning:** Firms should integrate sustainability metrics into core financial planning tools to optimize both risk exposure and growth potential.  
✔ **Operational Efficiency Focus:** Improving asset and inventory turnover metrics benefits both financial results and ESG performance narratives.  
✔ **Strategic Innovation Investment:** Investing in R&D and product development should be evaluated holistically — balancing long-term market positioning with current financial constraints.
